**Subject:** FrontierLab Research Internship Inquiry – Yu-Shyang Wei (NYCU)

Dear Professor Harada,

My name is Yu-Shyang Wei, a senior Mechanical Engineering student at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), Taiwan. I have recently been nominated for the FrontierLab exchange program and would like to inquire about the possibility of joining your laboratory as a visiting research student for one year starting from [September, 2026] to [September, 2027].

I am particularly interested in your research on human motion analysis and task/motion planning for robotic manipulation. Your approach of capturing and analyzing human dexterous manipulation and extracting essential features for robotic implementation is especially inspiring to me. I am also very interested in the integration of grasp planning, assembly sequence planning, and graph-based motion planning for autonomous robotic tasks.

My background focuses on reinforcement learning and robotic control. I have worked on robotic manipulation using a TM5-900 collaborative robot arm and developed perception-to-control integration using ROS. Through these projects, I became increasingly interested in how learning-based control and planning frameworks can be combined with structured task planning and motion generation.

I am especially motivated to explore how reinforcement learning or imitation-based approaches can complement task and motion planning to improve adaptability and robustness in real-world manipulation scenarios.

I would be honored to contribute to your laboratory’s research on robotic manipulation and planning. I have attached my CV for your reference and would sincerely appreciate the opportunity to discuss potential research directions under your supervision.

Thank you very much for your time and consideration.
