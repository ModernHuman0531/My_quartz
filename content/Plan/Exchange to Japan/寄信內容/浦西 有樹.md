Dear Professor Urushibara,

My name is Yu-Shyang Wei, a senior Mechanical Engineering student at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), Taiwan. I have been nominated for the FrontierLab exchange program and would like to inquire about the possibility of joining your laboratory as a visiting research student from September 2026 to September 2027.

I recently read your work on the Mixed Reality-based Robot Navigation Interface using Optical See-through MR-beacons (MRNaB), and I was particularly impressed by how your system enhances spatial understanding and interaction efficiency through persistent MR-beacons. The integration of real-world spatial anchors with robot navigation signals is a very elegant solution to overcoming the limitations of traditional 2D interfaces.

I am especially interested in how MR interfaces can bridge human intention and autonomous robot navigation. In dynamic real-world environments, interpreting user intent efficiently and translating it into robot motion planning remains a challenging problem. I believe MR-based interfaces open new possibilities for intuitive human-robot interaction.

My background focuses on reinforcement learning and robotic control. I have implemented SLAM-based navigation and obstacle avoidance for mobile robots using ROS, and developed perception-to-control pipelines for robotic manipulation systems. Through these experiences, I became strongly interested in integrating human interfaces, learning-based control, and real-world robotic systems.

If given the opportunity, I would be highly motivated to explore how learning-based approaches—such as reinforcement learning or adaptive planning—could be integrated with MR-based interfaces to improve navigation robustness and user adaptability.

I have attached my CV for your reference and would sincerely appreciate the opportunity to discuss possible research directions under your supervision before the FrontierLab application deadline on March 13, 2026.
