# content
During my high school years, I had the opportunity to participate in an exchange activity with students from Kumamoto, Japan. Through this interaction, I developed a strong interest in Japanese culture and began studying the Japanese language. Since then, I have hoped to experience studying abroad in Japan in order to broaden my academic perspective and deepen my cross-cultural understanding.

As a Mechanical Engineering student at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University, my primary academic interest lies in robotics. Japan has long been one of the world’s leading countries in robotics research and innovation, particularly in humanoid robots, legged robots, and robotic manipulation. Because of this strong research tradition, I have always hoped to learn and conduct research in Japan.

The University of Osaka is especially attractive to me because it hosts several leading laboratories in robotics. Their research spans a wide range of areas, including humanoid robotics, multi-legged robots, robot learning, and robotic manipulation. While studying at NYCU, I have gained experience in reinforcement learning, robot control, and ROS-based robotic systems. However, certain advanced robotic platforms and experimental resources are difficult to access at my home university. Therefore, studying at Osaka University would provide a valuable opportunity to work with real robotic hardware and to deepen my understanding of experimental robotics research.

Through the FrontierLab program, I hope to further develop my research abilities, gain hands-on experience with advanced robotic systems, and learn from leading researchers in the field. At the same time, I look forward to engaging in cultural exchange and building international academic connections that will support my future research career in robotics.
