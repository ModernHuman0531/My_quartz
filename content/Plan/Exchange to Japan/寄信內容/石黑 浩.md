### Research Plan
Recent advances in artificial intelligence and robotics have enabled robots to operate in increasingly complex environments. However, enabling robots to perceive, understand, and interact with human-centered environments remains a challenging problem. In particular, humanoid robots must integrate perception, spatial understanding, and motion control in order to function effectively in dynamic and unstructured environments. Therefore, my research interest focuses on developing intelligent perception and control systems for humanoid robots by integrating vision-based perception and learning-based decision making.

My academic background in mechanical engineering has provided me with a strong foundation in robotics and autonomous systems. During my undergraduate studies, I have studied subjects including robotics, automatic control, and machine learning. I have also gained practical experience working with robotic systems and software frameworks such as ROS. In addition, I have worked on projects involving computer vision and reinforcement learning for autonomous systems, which allowed me to develop skills in perception, algorithm design, and system integration. These experiences have prepared me to work on research problems that require the integration of perception, learning, and robotic control.

For my proposed research, I am particularly interested in exploring vision-based perception and spatial understanding for humanoid robots. Humanoid robots such as the Unitree G1 are designed to operate in environments built for humans, which requires the ability to perceive objects, understand spatial relationships, and navigate complex surroundings. I am interested in developing a vision–motor system that enables humanoid robots to interpret visual information and translate it into meaningful actions. Such a system may include modules for visual perception, environment representation, and motion planning, allowing the robot to autonomously navigate and interact with its environment.

Another aspect of my interest is the integration of learning-based approaches with robot perception and control. Traditional robotics systems often rely on carefully designed models and rules, but recent advances in machine learning provide new opportunities to enable robots to learn from data and improve their behavior through experience. I am particularly interested in exploring how learning-based methods such as reinforcement learning can be combined with perception systems to enhance the adaptability and intelligence of humanoid robots. This integration could allow robots to better understand complex environments and perform tasks more robustly in real-world scenarios.

Through this research, I hope to contribute to the development of intelligent humanoid robots that can operate safely and effectively in human environments. More broadly, I believe that combining artificial intelligence with robotics will play an important role in the future development of service robots and autonomous systems.
